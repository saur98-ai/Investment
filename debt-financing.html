What Is Debt Financing?
Debt financing allows businesses and individuals to raise capital by borrowing against future cash flows or assets — without giving up equity ownership. Structured debt products like NCDs, debentures, and credit facilities offer fixed returns for investors and predictable costs for borrowers.
Key advantage: Raise capital while retaining 100% ownership of your business

Step 03 — Suitability
Business · HNI · Promoter · Income Investor
Is Debt Financing Right for You?
Debt financing suits businesses seeking expansion capital without dilution, promoters needing liquidity, and investors looking for superior fixed-income returns above bank FDs. For portfolio investors, structured debt adds stability and predictable income — particularly valuable in volatile equity markets.
Portfolio tip: Allocating 20–30% of a HNI portfolio to structured debt significantly reduces volatility
