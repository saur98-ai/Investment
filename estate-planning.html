Step 01 — The Big Picture

Estate Planning Is More Than a Will
Estate planning is the process of arranging your affairs so that your wealth transfers to the right people, at the right time, in the most efficient way possible. It combines a Will (for direction), a Trust (for control), nominations (for speed), insurance (for liquidity), and tax planning (to preserve value) into one coherent strategy.
Key insight: A Will alone can take 3–5 years through probate — a Trust transfer happens in days

Step 02 — The 5 Pillars
Will · Trust · Nominees · Insurance · Tax
A Complete Estate Plan Covers Five Areas
A Will gives legal direction. A Trust enables control and probate-free transfer. Nominations ensure quick access to liquid assets. Insurance provides immediate liquidity for estate expenses and dependents. Tax planning ensures your heirs keep more of what you leave — not the taxman. All five must work in harmony.
Most neglected: Updating nominations after marriage, divorce, or having children — check yours today

6 Estate Planning Mistakes We Fix
Most Indian families have significant gaps in their estate plans — no Will, outdated nominees on old accounts, no liquidity for funeral/probate costs, unregistered property titles, and digital assets (crypto, passwords) that disappear with the owner. We audit all of this and fill every gap systematically.
