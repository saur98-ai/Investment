Term Insurance: The Most Important Product You're Skipping

📅 May 2025 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ Akshay Maheshwari

If you have a family depending on your income — and you do not have a term insurance policy — you are carrying one of the biggest financial risks a person can carry. Not because markets are volatile. Not because inflation is rising. But because if something happens to you tomorrow, the people you love will face a financial crisis you could have prevented for less than ₹1,200 a month.

Term insurance is the single most important financial product most Indians are either skipping entirely or replacing with something far inferior.

What Is Term Insurance and Why Is It So Affordable?

Term insurance is pure life protection. You pay a premium for a defined period (say, 30 years). If you pass away during that period, your family receives the sum assured. If you survive, the policy expires with no maturity benefit. That simplicity is exactly why it is so cheap — there is no investment component, no savings element, no cash value. Just protection.

A healthy 30-year-old non-smoker can buy ₹1 Crore of cover for a 30-year term at roughly ₹800–₹1,200 per month. That is less than a family dinner out. And it means your family receives ₹1 Crore if the worst happens.

How Much Cover Do You Actually Need?

A common rule of thumb: your term insurance cover should be 10–15 times your annual income. If you earn ₹12 Lakhs per year, you need ₹1.2–1.8 Crore of cover. This ensures your family can replace your income stream without financial stress.

Also account for:

  • Outstanding loans (home loan, car loan, personal loan)
  • Children's education costs
  • Spouse's retirement needs
  • Any business liabilities

Add these up, subtract your existing investments and assets, and that is your true insurance gap. For most working professionals in India, the gap is between ₹1 Crore and ₹2 Crore.

"The best investment strategy and the best insurance strategy are rarely the same product. Keep them separate."

ULIPs and endowment plans try to combine insurance and investment — and end up doing both poorly. The insurance cover is inadequate, and the investment returns are subpar after deducting heavy charges. Term insurance + SIP is always the superior combination.

Useful Riders to Consider

Riders are add-ons to your base term policy that enhance coverage at a small additional cost:

  • Critical Illness Rider: Pays a lump sum on diagnosis of cancer, heart attack, stroke, etc. (typically 34–60 conditions covered)
  • Accidental Death Benefit: Pays additional cover if death is due to an accident
  • Waiver of Premium: Future premiums are waived if you become disabled and unable to work
  • Terminal Illness Rider: Advances the death benefit if you are diagnosed with a terminal illness

The Critical Illness rider, in particular, is highly recommended — medical costs in India are rising 12–15% annually, and a serious diagnosis can drain savings even when the patient survives.

3 Myths About Term Insurance — Busted

Myth 1: "I'm young and healthy, I don't need it yet"

This is exactly when you should buy it. Premiums are lowest when you are young and healthy. A 25-year-old pays roughly 40% less premium than a 35-year-old for the same cover. And health conditions that develop later can either make you uninsurable or dramatically increase your premium.

Myth 2: "My company gives me life cover"

Group life cover from employers is typically 3–5 times annual salary — far below the 10–15x you need. It also disappears the day you leave the job, and you cannot port it to a personal policy without fresh underwriting.

Myth 3: "Term insurance has no returns, so it's a waste"

This is like saying a car's brakes are a waste because you hope never to use them. The purpose of insurance is protection, not returns. Separate your investment and protection goals. Use SIPs for wealth creation, use term insurance for family protection. Both are essential.

Key Takeaway

A ₹1 Crore term insurance policy costs just ₹800–1,200/month for a healthy 30-year-old — less than a family dinner. Your cover should be 10–15x annual income. Term insurance is always superior to ULIPs and endowment plans for protection. Buy young, buy early, and keep it simple.
