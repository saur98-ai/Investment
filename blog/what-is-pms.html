What is PMS and Should You Invest in It?

📅 May 2025 ⏱ 6 min read ✍️ Akshay Maheshwari

You have been hearing about PMS in conversations at dinner parties and family gatherings. A colleague mentions their ₹50 Lakh portfolio managed by a specialist. A business acquaintance talks about alpha generation and concentrated bets. But nobody quite explains what it is, how it actually works, or whether it belongs in your financial plan. Let's demystify it.

What Is Portfolio Management Service (PMS)?

A Portfolio Management Service is a SEBI-regulated investment service where a professional portfolio manager constructs and manages a personalised portfolio of stocks and other securities on your behalf. Unlike a mutual fund where thousands of investors pool their money into one common portfolio, PMS gives you an individual, segregated account — your securities are held directly in your name in your own Demat account.

SEBI mandates a minimum investment of ₹50 Lakhs for PMS. This high entry threshold ensures PMS is designed for High Net Worth Individuals (HNIs) who can absorb concentrated risk and have surplus capital beyond their core financial plan.

Discretionary vs Non-Discretionary PMS

PMS comes in two main flavours:

  • Discretionary PMS: The portfolio manager makes all buy/sell decisions independently. You give them full authority to manage the portfolio within the agreed mandate. This is the most common and hands-off form.
  • Non-Discretionary PMS: The portfolio manager recommends trades, but you approve each transaction. You retain control but it is more time-consuming. Suitable for investors who want to stay closely involved.
"PMS is not just an investment product — it is a relationship with a professional who has genuine skin in the game and a deep understanding of your financial goals."

When Does PMS Make Sense?

PMS is not right for everyone who has ₹50 Lakhs. Here are the situations where it genuinely adds value:

  • You want concentrated, high-conviction bets: PMS managers often run 15–25 stock portfolios with strong views, aiming to meaningfully outperform the index
  • Your existing MF portfolio is too large to diversify further: Beyond ₹2–3 Crore in mutual funds, a PMS can offer genuine differentiation
  • You have specific sector expertise: Some investors prefer thematic PMS strategies aligned to industries they understand
  • You want custom tax planning: PMS allows tax-loss harvesting and year-end rebalancing in ways mutual funds cannot offer
  • You have concentrated stock positions: A PMS manager can systematically diversify a large single-stock holding (e.g., from an ESOP or inheritance)

The Risks and Costs You Must Understand

PMS carries higher costs and higher concentration risk than mutual funds. Be clear-eyed about this:

  • Management fees: Typically 1.5–2.5% per year, charged on the total portfolio value — significantly higher than most equity mutual funds (0.5–1.5%)
  • Performance fees: Many PMS providers charge 10–20% of gains above a hurdle rate (e.g., Nifty benchmark). This aligns interest but reduces your net returns in good years
  • Concentration risk: A 20-stock portfolio means each stock is 5% of your wealth. One bad call significantly affects you
  • Track record is short: Many PMS funds have only 3–5 years of history, often through a bull market. Evaluate with caution

Alternatives to Consider

If PMS interests you but the ₹50L minimum or risk profile gives you pause, consider:

  • AIF (Alternative Investment Fund) — Category III: For investors with ₹1 Crore+, offering hedge-fund-style strategies
  • Smallcase: Professionally curated model portfolios executed in your Demat account, starting at ₹5,000–50,000
  • Direct equity with advisor guidance: Build a focused portfolio with an advisor's research support at lower cost
  • Multi-cap or flexi-cap mutual funds: Well-managed active funds can deliver near-PMS returns at a fraction of the cost below the ₹50L threshold

PMS is a powerful tool in the right hands, for the right investor. The key is matching the product to your financial stage, risk appetite, and wealth complexity — not just your net worth.

Key Takeaway

PMS requires a minimum ₹50 Lakh investment and offers individualised, concentrated equity portfolios managed by a professional. It suits HNIs looking for personalisation, tax flexibility, and concentrated bets — but comes with higher fees and concentration risk. Evaluate PMS only as a complement to, not a replacement for, a well-diversified core portfolio.
