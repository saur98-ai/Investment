The ₹2.8 Crore Difference Between Starting SIP at 25 vs 35

📅 May 2025 ⏱ 4 min read ✍️ Akshay Maheshwari

Here is a question most people never seriously ask: what is the cost of waiting 10 years to start investing? Not in abstract terms — in actual rupees. The answer is almost always shocking, and it is the single most powerful argument for starting a SIP today, even if the amount feels small.

The Numbers That Change Everything

Let's take a simple example: ₹5,000 per month, invested in an equity mutual fund with a 12% annual return. Compare Person A who starts at 25, and Person B who starts at 35 — both stopping at age 60.

Scenario Monthly SIP Period Total Invested Final Corpus
Start at 25₹5,00035 years₹21 Lakhs₹3.5 Crore
Start at 35₹5,00025 years₹15 Lakhs₹70 Lakhs
The GapSame amount10 years less₹6 Lakhs extra₹2.8 Crore lost

Person A invested just ₹6 Lakhs more in total — but walked away with ₹2.8 Crore more. That 10-year head start did not just add 10 years of returns. It gave those early investments 35 years to compound, and those final 10 years of compounding are where most of the wealth is created.

"Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it. He who doesn't, pays it." — Albert Einstein

Why the Last 10 Years Do Most of the Work

This is the counterintuitive heart of compounding. In Person A's journey from 25 to 60:

  • After 10 years (age 35): corpus is approximately ₹11.6 Lakhs
  • After 20 years (age 45): corpus is approximately ₹49.9 Lakhs
  • After 30 years (age 55): corpus is approximately ₹1.76 Crore
  • After 35 years (age 60): corpus is approximately ₹3.5 Crore

Notice that in the last 5 years alone (55 to 60), the corpus nearly doubles — from ₹1.76 Crore to ₹3.5 Crore. The first 20 years build the foundation; the last 15 years create the fortune. Person B who starts at 35 misses exactly this exponential phase.

The Step-Up SIP: The Secret Multiplier

Now imagine adding just one more discipline: increase your SIP by 10% every year. This is called a step-up SIP or top-up SIP, and it aligns your investment growth with your natural salary growth.

Starting at ₹5,000/month at age 25, with a 10% annual step-up, at 12% returns over 35 years:

  • Flat ₹5,000 SIP: ₹3.5 Crore
  • 10% step-up SIP: ₹10.8 Crore

The step-up SIP delivers nearly 3x the corpus for a fraction of additional effort. By year 35, you would be investing approximately ₹87,000 per month — but by then, salary growth over a career typically makes this very manageable.

What if You Are 35 Already? Not All Is Lost.

If you are reading this at 35, do not despair. The best time to plant a tree was 10 years ago; the second-best time is now. At 35 with 25 years to go, ₹10,000/month still becomes ₹1.4 Crore at 12%. Add a step-up, and it becomes ₹3.8 Crore. The numbers are still powerful — they just require a slightly higher commitment.

The only move that is guaranteed to fail is waiting another year. Every 12 months of delay reduces your final corpus by roughly 12–15% in the critical early stages.

Getting Started Is Simpler Than You Think

A SIP can be started with as little as ₹500 per month. You do not need a perfect fund selection, a perfect market entry point, or a perfect financial plan. A simple diversified equity fund, started today, with automatic monthly debit, and a commitment to not stop it when markets fall — that is 90% of the strategy.

The 10% of refinement — fund selection, asset allocation, step-up planning, tax optimisation — can be done with a good advisor. But the most important decision is simply to begin.

Key Takeaway

₹5,000/month from age 25 grows to ₹3.5 Crore by 60. Starting at 35 yields only ₹70 Lakhs — a gap of ₹2.8 Crore from just a 10-year delay. Add a 10% annual step-up and the same SIP from age 25 reaches ₹10.8 Crore. The most powerful financial decision you can make is to start today.
