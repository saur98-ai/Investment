Step 01 — Understanding AIF
SEBI Regulated · Pooled · Alternative
What is an Alternative Investment Fund?
An AIF is a privately pooled investment vehicle that collects funds from sophisticated investors and invests in non-traditional assets — venture capital, hedge funds, private debt, real estate, and more. SEBI regulates all AIFs in India.
Key fact: AIF minimum investment is ₹1 Crore — designed for serious wealth

Step 02 — Three Categories
Cat I · Cat II · Cat III
Which AIF Category Fits You?
Category I funds back startups and social ventures. Category II includes PE and real estate debt funds — the most popular. Category III covers hedge funds using complex trading strategies for absolute returns.
