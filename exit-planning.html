Why Exit Planning Can't Be Last-Minute
Most business owners start thinking about exit only when they're ready to leave — and that's too late. Valuation multiples depend on 2–3 years of clean financials, governance structures, and growth trajectories. Tax liabilities on business sale can be 20–30% without proper planning. Step 02 — Exit Routes
Strategic Sale · IPO · MBO · PE Buyout
Which Exit Route Is Right for You?
Strategic sales to industry buyers often yield the highest premiums. IPOs provide liquidity while retaining partial ownership. Management buyouts (MBO) allow the business to stay in trusted hands. Private equity buyouts bring capital and professionalization. Each route has different timelines, tax implications, and outcomes for you and your family.
Best returns: Strategic sales typically command 20–40% premium over PE buyouts

Step 03 — Tax Strategy
Capital Gains · Indexation · 54EC · Structuring
Minimise Tax on Your Business Sale
The difference between a share sale and slump sale can mean crores in tax difference. We model the full tax impact across every exit route — capital gains (LTCG/STCG), indexation benefits, Section 54EC reinvestment in bonds, ESOP liquidation, and whether a Trust or holding company structure reduces your effective tax rate significantly.
