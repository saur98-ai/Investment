Step 01 — The Imperative

Why Every Family Business Needs a Succession Plan
In India, over 90% of businesses are family-run, yet fewer than 30% have a formal succession plan. Without one, leadership transitions become battles — between siblings, between generations, between family and professional management. A structured succession plan prevents disputes and ensures continuity.

Step 02 — Business Transition
Leadership · Ownership · Governance · Constitution
Planning Business Succession
Effective business succession has two components — leadership and ownership, which don't always go to the same person. We help you identify successors, build governance structures (Family Councils, Boards), transfer shareholding tax-efficiently, and draft a Family Constitution that sets rules for future generations to follow.
Key distinction: Leadership and ownership can — and often should — be separated

Step 03 — Wealth Transition

Transferring Personal Wealth to the Next Generation
Beyond the business, personal wealth — investments, properties, insurance, gold, digital assets — needs a clear transition plan.
