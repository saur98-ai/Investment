What Is a Private Trust?
A Trust is a legal arrangement where a Settlor transfers assets to a Trustee to hold and manage for designated Beneficiaries. In India, Private Trusts are governed by the Indian Trusts Act, 1882 and offer unmatched control over how wealth is preserved and distributed across generations.
Key insight: A Trust continues beyond your lifetime — assets stay protected without going through probate

Step 02 — Structure Options
Revocable · Irrevocable · Discretionary · Charitable
Which Type of Trust Suits You?
Revocable Trusts offer flexibility during your lifetime. Irrevocable Trusts provide the strongest protection from creditors and tax efficiency. Discretionary Trusts give trustees flexibility in distribution timing. Charitable Trusts combine giving with tax deductions under Section 80G.
Most popular: Discretionary Irrevocable Trusts for HNI families seeking succession certainty

Step 03 — Why a Trust Works
Protect · Preserve · Transfer · Control
Advantages of Setting Up a Trust
Unlike a Will, a Trust takes effect immediately, avoids probate entirely, and keeps your financial affairs private. Assets held in trust are protected from creditors and family disputes. Income can be distributed tax-efficiently across family members in lower tax brackets.
Key advantage: Trust assets are outside your personal estate — shielded from claims and court delays
